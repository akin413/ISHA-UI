The JAST-V Core: A 4D Emotional Compass

Unlike simple systems that only see "Happy" or "Angry," GPRF uses a scientific, multidimensional map to capture the full spectrum of human feeling.

The Four Bipolar Dimensions

Our system maps your input onto four continuous, opposing axes, based on Plutchik's Theory. This allows us to detect subtle, mixed, and nuanced emotions.

Joy ↔ Sadness
Anger ↔ Fear
Surprise ↔ Anticipation
Trust ↔ Disgust

Why 4D?

Imagine saying, "I'm excited to finish this project, but I also dread the presentation." GPRF doesn't categorize this as just "mixed." It scores high on Anticipation (Joy/Sadness axis) but also high on Fear (Anger/Fear axis), resulting in a precise Emotional Vector that guides the AI's reply.

This level of nuance is key to achieving truly social AI.
